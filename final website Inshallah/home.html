Achievements

Between 1985 and 2000, Nasrallah led numerous military operations against the Israeli occupation, which resulted in Israel's complete withdrawal from southern Lebanon without reaching any agreement or treaty with Lebanon.

He led a war known as the 33-day war in 2006, following Hezbollah's capture of two Israeli soldiers in Operation True Promise, and they achieved a resounding victory, were it not for the Zionist entity's rush to the UN Security Council to issue Resolution 1701.

He actively intervened in what is called the Syrian war in defense of Lady Zainab, peace be upon her, despite all that has been said about this strife.

During the Battle of Al-Aqsa, he categorically refused to cease fire from the Lebanese side until the aggression against Gaza was halted, until his martyrdom.
